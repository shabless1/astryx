## LAYER 1 — THE FREQUENCY LOCK (non-negotiable)

The Tone.js engine plays the planet's exact Cousto Hz as a **drone on the root note**. With no drums, the lock lives entirely in the **key + that drone**. Three rules:

1. **Root every chord on the planet's note.** Start and resolve there. That note = the Hz. Never transpose, never modulate.
2. **Stay inside the mode.** Only the notes of the planet's mode. No chromatic wandering (except deliberate dark-soul tones on Pluto/Saturn).
3. **Voice chords as soul extensions — 7, 9, 11, 13, 6/9, sus2, add9.** These extensions are *overtones of the root*. They give all the neo-soul lushness while **reinforcing** the fundamental Hz.

> The soul lives in the extensions. The frequency lives in the root. Stack soul on root = both.

**Tempo:** with no beat, tempo is only a notional pacing for the chords. Use `slow, free-time, unhurried` (a loose 55–70 BPM feel). The frequency is carried by the key and the drone, not a pulse.

## LAYER 2 — THE FLOW (drumless, spacious, slow)

No drums. No percussion of any kind. Motion comes from *tonal* sources, never a beat. The track should feel like it's floating and breathing.

**Flow cheat-codes (the words that make Suno relax and open):**
```
ambient, drumless, no percussion, free-time, slow, unhurried, spacious,
long sustained chords, one chord per bar or longer, slow-moving harmony,
floating, let it breathe, lots of space, gentle swells, warm pads underneath,
long reverb tails, tape warmth, meditative, soothing
```

**How to imply gentle movement without drums:**
- **Rhodes / harp / kora arpeggios** — soft, rolling tonal figures (melodic, not rhythmic).
- **Pad swells** — strings or analog pads that rise and fall like breath.
- **Bass note motion** — long sustained bass moving slowly between chord roots.
- **Kalimba / vibraphone sparkle** — occasional, spacious, never a pattern.

> If you want a faint pulse, let it come from a *slow Rhodes arpeggio* or *swelling pad* — never a kick or snare.

## LAYER 3 — THE WARMTH PALETTE (tribal · ethnic · Afro-chill · soulful)

This is where the soul lives. The **tribal / Afro feel comes from ethnic MELODIC instruments and wordless chant — never from drums.** Blend an ETHNIC PULSE + a SOUL CORE + an EARTH DRONE for the full ASTRYX Afro-chill signature.

| ETHNIC PULSE (the tribal warmth, drumless) | SOUL CORE | EARTH DRONE + AMBIENT |
|--------------------------------------------|-----------|------------------------|
| **kora** (West African harp) | fender rhodes, wurlitzer | didgeridoo earth drone |
| **mbira / kalimba** (thumb piano) | Hammond organ (slow Leslie) | tanpura / shruti drone |
| **balafon** (warm wood xylophone) | muted trumpet, soft sax | warm analog synth pad |
| **ngoni / nyatiti** (West African lute) | upright bass (sustained) | deep tape & vinyl warmth |
| **wordless chant / hum / overtone** (no words) | lush strings, vibraphone | wide deep reverb, space |

**The tribal-soul secret:** kora, mbira, kalimba and balafon give a gentle, hypnotic, *rhythmic-melodic* motion that reads as "Afro / tribal" while staying soft and soothing — the pulse of a drum with none of the hit. Layer a low **wordless chant** under them for ancestral soul.

**Wordless vocal texture is allowed and encouraged** — humming, chanting, overtone tones as *texture only*. Strictly **no words, no lyrics, no lead vocal.**

**Production words:** warm, dusty, earthy, ancestral, sun-warmed, vinyl, analog, deep reverb, sacred, spacious, grounded.
